Ageless Wonder: Ronaldo’s Masterclass Secures Second Nations League Title for Portugal

At 40 years old, the five‑time Ballon d’Or winner remains not just relevant—but decisive, adding another chapter to his legendary career.

Cristiano Ronaldo has added yet another chapter to his legendary career, guiding Portugal to a dramatic victory in the 2024–25 UEFA Nations League. At 40 years old, the five‑time Ballon d’Or winner remains not just relevant—but decisive.

A Historic Night in Munich

In a pulsating final at Munich’s Allianz Arena, Portugal faced Spain in what became an instant classic. Spain took the lead twice—first via Martín Zubimendi and then Mikel Oyarzabal—only for Portugal to rally. Nuno Mendes equalized before halftime and later set up Ronaldo’s thunderous volley in the 61st minute, forcing the match into extra time. Neither team could break the deadlock, leading to a penalty shootout where Portugal triumphed 5–3, clinching the title as the first nation to win the Nations League twice.

Ronaldo: The Undisputed Leader

Ronaldo scored his 138th international goal to level the final, extending his all‑time scoring record for Portugal. Though he exited injured in the 88th minute, he watched the shootout with raw emotion. Footage shows him collapsing onto the turf, tears streaming as his teammates secured the win.

“Winning with the national team is always something very special… These are tears. Mission accomplished and so much joy.”

This triumph marks his third major international title—after UEFA Euro 2016 and the 2019 Nations League—and his second Nations League crown as captain, solidifying his unparalleled legacy.

Tactical Brilliance & Collective Resolve

  • Coach Martínez’s Winning Philosophy: Roberto Martínez, Portugal’s manager since January 2023, has cultivated a high-performing, tight-knit squad with a remarkable 70% win rate— the best for any Portuguese national team coach. He praised Ronaldo’s influence, noting his mix of “unique experience” and exceptional goalscoring form: 20 in 25 recent matches.
  • Mendes, Defensive Anchor & Set-Piece Threat: A standout performer, Nuno Mendes not only neutralized Spain’s young star Lamine Yamal, but also opened the scoring and helped engineer Ronaldo’s equalizer. His composure was decisive in the shootout, earning him Player of the Match honors.
  • Penalty Drama & Costa’s Crucial Save: Diogo Costa was the hero between the sticks, saving Morata’s effort during the shootout. With flawless execution from his teammates, Portugal held strong to claim victory.

Legacy, Future & Ronaldo’s Uncertain Next Steps

This emotional victory reaffirmed Ronaldo’s undiminished hunger to excel for Portugal. While speculation swirls around his club future—prompted by contract talks with Al‑Nassr and interest from clubs in the U.S. and beyond—Ronaldo insisted he remains focused on rest and recovery following this triumph.

The Bigger Picture

Portugal’s win reinforces their growing dominance in the Nations League. As fans look ahead to Euro 2028 and another World Cup cycle, Ronaldo’s enduring presence will again be central to their ambitions.
